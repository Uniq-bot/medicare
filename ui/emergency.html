When to Go to the Emergency Room

Life-Threatening Symptoms

  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Sudden severe headache
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Severe bleeding that won't stop
  • Signs of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
  • Severe allergic reaction
  • Poisoning or overdose

Urgent Symptoms

  • High fever with rash
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Deep cuts requiring stitches
  • Suspected broken bones
  • Severe burns
  • Head injury with confusion
  • Severe vomiting or diarrhea
  • Eye injuries

Consider Urgent Care For

  • Minor cuts and burns
  • Sprains and strains
  • Ear or throat pain
  • Minor allergic reactions
  • Cold and flu symptoms
  • Mild fever
  • Urinary tract infections
  • Rashes without fever

What to Expect in the ER

1

Arrival & Triage

You'll be assessed by a triage nurse who will prioritize cases based on severity

2

Registration

Provide your information, insurance details, and medical history

3

Medical Evaluation

Doctor examines you and orders necessary tests or imaging

4

Treatment

Receive immediate care, medication, or procedures as needed

5

Discharge or Admission

Go home with care instructions or be admitted to the hospital

How to Prepare for an ER Visit

Bring With You

  • Photo ID and insurance card
  • List of current medications
  • List of allergies
  • Medical history summary
  • Recent test results or X-rays

Be Ready to Tell Us

  • When symptoms started
  • What makes symptoms better or worse
  • Your pain level (1-10 scale)
  • Any recent injuries or illnesses
  • Contact information for your primary doctor
